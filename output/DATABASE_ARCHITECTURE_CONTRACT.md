1. DATABASE DOCTRINE
Database Doctrine

The database exists exclusively as persistence infrastructure.

The database preserves authoritative outcomes generated by authoritative systems.

The database is not an authority system.

The database never owns business truth.

The database never creates business truth.

The database never determines business truth.

Authority exists independently of database implementation.

Database replacement must never alter authoritative business behavior.

Persistence Doctrine

Persistence exists to preserve:

authoritative state
authoritative events
authoritative evidence
authoritative history
authoritative lineage

Persistence is subordinate to authority.

Persistence follows authority.

Persistence never defines authority.

Authority Separation Doctrine

Authority and persistence are independent concerns.

Authority determines:

what happened
why it happened
who caused it
when it became valid

Persistence only records the outcome.

Databases must never become authority substitutes.

Transaction Doctrine

Transactions preserve authoritative outcomes.

Transactions never determine authoritative outcomes.

Transaction completion does not create authority.

Authority must exist before persistence occurs.

Historical Preservation Doctrine

Historical truth must survive:

software replacement
database replacement
infrastructure replacement
operational failure
recovery procedures

Historical preservation is mandatory.

Historical deletion is exceptional.

Reconstruction Doctrine

Every persisted fact must be reconstructable.

Every reconstructed fact must be traceable.

Every reconstruction must be deterministic.

Replay-Safe Persistence Doctrine

Persistence must support:

replay
recovery
rehydration
forensic reconstruction

Replay must reproduce identical authoritative outcomes.

What Databases Are

Databases are:

persistence systems
storage systems
retrieval systems
reconstruction systems
What Databases Are Not

Databases are not:

authority systems
business engines
billing engines
payment engines
enforcement engines
lifecycle engines
